That growth came from acquisition and hard work — and both have a ceiling when the operations underneath aren't standardized. Here's the operational audit, the way you'd run it: each bottleneck, what it's quietly costing, and what a system does about it.

🧩

Acquisition integration is manual.

Every brand runs on Service Titan — but each one was set up its own way. Bringing a new acquisition onto one standard, with matching workflows and reporting, still happens by hand, and mismatched setups are what a buyer's diligence team marks down.

Cost: slower integrations · a softer multiple at exit
⚖️

Headcount isn't matched to output.

One acquired company runs four technicians and six office staff. People are "busy" but you can't see who's actually moving the needle — "stuck in the mud all day."

Cost: overlapping payroll · EBITDA left on the table
📐

Estimating output swings wildly.

One estimator ships 12 bids a day; four others manage four between them — on cookie-cutter work that gets rebuilt from scratch every time instead of templated and priced consistently.

Cost: bids lost to slow turnaround · margin variance
📊

No live view across the brands.

You can spot a "pop" in the numbers months later and not know what caused it. There's no shared scoreboard, so the strong performers and the laggards blur together.

Cost: "you can't measure what you don't track"
📞

Every missed call is a lost job.

It's a phone tree today — no automation. When a call is missed or comes after hours, nothing catches it. In emergency access work, that's revenue walking to the next number on the list.

Cost: booked jobs lost before anyone calls back
⭐

Invisible where buyers now look.

19 years in, and an ex-employee has 700 Google reviews to your handful. That's the signal Google ranks on and the signal AI search reads when someone asks "best access-control company near me."

Cost: leads routed to competitors · weaker brand equity

None of this is a revenue problem. It's an operational-visibility problem — and operational visibility is exactly what expands, or compresses, your multiple.
