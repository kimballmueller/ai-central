01 — Where You Are

A real business in a competitive market.

Powerhouse Roofing wins jobs on quality and reputation. But residential roofing is one of the most contested markets in home services — homeowners shop online before they ever pick up the phone, ad costs climb every season, and the contractor who shows up first and follows up fastest usually wins the job. The work is excellent; the growth engine around it can be sharper.

"You do not rise to the level of your goals. You fall to the level of your systems."
— James Clear, Atomic Habits

Most roofing companies grow on referrals and hustle — and hustle has a ceiling. As you scale, the cracks show up in the same three places every time:

🔍

Hard to find where it counts.

When a homeowner Googles "roof replacement near me" — or asks ChatGPT who to call — you need to be the name that comes up. Thin search visibility means the biggest jobs go to whoever shows up first.

💧

Leads leaking out the bottom.

The vast majority of website visitors leave without calling. With no retargeting in place, those homeowners — who were this close — are gone for good and pick a competitor instead.

📣

Marketing that depends on you.

Content, ads, and follow-up live in scattered tools and a few people's heads. Nothing runs on a system, so output stalls the moment the team gets busy on the roof.

None of this is a quality problem. It's a systems problem — and systems are fixable.
