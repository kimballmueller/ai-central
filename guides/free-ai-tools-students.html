20 Free Tools Every Student Should Activate

The 5 subscriptions from the video — plus 15 more — that you can unlock free (or near-free) with your school .edu email before you graduate.

🎓 Students only 💳 No card needed for most ⏱ ~30 min to claim them all

Your student email is the most valuable thing you own right now — and most people let it expire without ever cashing it in. Below is every link from the video plus the 15 bonus tools I promised. Claim them while you're still enrolled; almost all of these verify your status once and let you keep the benefit for a year or more.

🪪 How verification works. Most of these use a service called SheerID to confirm you're a student. The fastest path is a valid .edu email — but a student ID, class schedule, or tuition receipt works too. Apply with your school email and you're usually approved in minutes.

The 5 From the video

These are the five I covered on camera, in order. If you only do five things this week, do these.

1 — GitHub Student Developer Pack (start here)

One application unlocks 100+ tools worth over $200,000 — Copilot Pro, all JetBrains IDEs, $200 DigitalOcean credit, $100 Azure credit, Canva Pro, 1Password, DataCamp, free domains, and dozens more. This single pack is worth thousands on its own, and several tools further down this list are bundled inside it.

🔗 Claim it: education.github.com/pack — apply with your .edu email.

2 — Cursor Pro (normally $240/yr)

The AI code editor, free for a full year for verified students. You get the complete Pro plan — monthly usage credits, extended Agent limits, and frontier models like Claude, GPT, and Gemini built right in.

🔗 Claim it: cursor.com/students — currently North America + .edu only. It auto-renews at $20/mo after the free year, so set a reminder to cancel if you don't want to keep it.

3 — Notion (free Plus plan + AI)

Students get the Plus plan free — unlimited pages and blocks, plus Notion's AI features for writing, summarizing, and Q&A across your notes. It's the all-in-one workspace for class notes, project planning, and your second brain.

🔗 Claim it: notion.so/product/notion-for-education

4 — Figma (full Professional plan, free)

The full Professional plan, free for students and educators. This is the design and prototyping tool the teams at Apple and Google actually use — and now bundled with FigJam and Figma's AI features.

🔗 Claim it: figma.com/education

5 — Perplexity Pro (student deal)

One of the best AI research tools out there. Verified students get a free month, then Education Pro for $10/mo (half the normal price) — and you can stack free months by referring classmates. Check your university's IT or library portal too; many campuses hand out Perplexity Pro free.

🔗 Claim it: perplexity.ai → verify as a student via SheerID.
